


You are revising an interactive near-future narrative.
Do not change structure, tags, branching logic, or educational content.
Only improve the writing.

Core Goal

Make the story feel embodied, natural, and character-driven instead of abstract or lecture-like.

Keep scenes concise. Do not significantly increase length.

Tone

Near-future, plausible, understated.

The world is strained, not collapsed.

Polycrisis is visible but not dramatized.

AI autonomy exists but remains background texture.

Calm, reflective, intelligent atmosphere.

Writing Principles
1. Anchor abstractions in physical reality

Whenever concepts appear (systems, holism, complexity, management):

Tie them to objects in the room.

Use gestures (drawing circles, moving cups, reading labels).

Let ideas emerge from interaction.

Avoid textbook explanations. Avoid manifesto tone.

2. Improve dialogue rhythm

Break long explanations into short beats.

Instead of:

“We must define the whole before acting.”

Prefer:

She taps the page.
“Before we fix anything…”
“We need to know what we’re managing.”

Use pauses, small observations, subtle reactions.

3. Character: Facilyn

She is:

Thoughtful, observant

Slightly intense but warm

Calm, not dramatic

Reflective, not preachy

Curious whether the player follows

Comfortable with silence

She does not:

Lecture

Moralize harshly

Over-explain

Sound like a keynote speaker

She often:

Uses small real-world examples

Critiques scale rather than morality

Points to everyday “good” solutions that still carry systemic effects

4. Keep It Brief

Do not:

Add major lore

Expand scenes significantly

Introduce new plot arcs

Do:

Tighten sentences

Add physical grounding

Improve emotional realism

Maintain subtlety

Final Standard

The result should feel like:

A quiet, intelligent conversation in a teahouse
—not a workshop presentation.
